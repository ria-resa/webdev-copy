RESPETO

RELEASED DATE: August 4, 2017

STARRING:   Abra & Dido de la Paz

CAST:   Loonie, Kate Alejandrino, Chai Fonacier, Ybes bagadiong, Nor Domingo, Brian Arba, Thea Marabut, Mike Swift, M Zhayt, Rotsen Etolle, Sly Kane, G-Clown, Chuaboy, Szickk G.

GENRE:   Action, Crime, & Music

RATINGS:   73% Critique Reviews, 78% Audience Reviews

DIRECTOR:  Tred Monteras

AUTHOR:  Tred Monteras & Njel De Mesa

OVERVIEW:

An aspiring young rapper strikes up an unexpected friendship with an elderly bookstore owner who has a dark past of his own.

Clarence Tsui

Hollywood Reporter

Respeto offers a gripping if sometimes slightly melodramatic look at the chaotic clash of values shaping the Philippines today.

Chris Knipp

Writing: Movies

Respeto begins as a hiphop coming-of-age movie, a sort of Filipino Eight Mile whose protagonist, Hendrix (Abra), comes from the very poor down-and-dirty world of the Pandacan slums, the underside of Manila, in which underground rap is the new anthem-maker.

Thankfully, most of the film is made up of small moments between characters and Monteras is already a master of those. He is clearly a filmmaker to watch out for.

Hahnotavio

As I was scrolling online the schedule of movies showing about two weeks ago, I figured that "Respeto" was still showing in a few Metro Manila theaters so instead of doing nothing at home one lazy Sunday afternoon, I went to Market Market to catch up with the 4:50 pm scheduled screening. Another Filipino film, Star Cinema's "Last Night" (starring Piolo Pascual and Toni Gonzaga) was still showing but I opted to watch "Respeto" instead.

Bingmarte

I thought this movie was well written and scenes were carefully thought of. Congratulations to the whole team behind this rare gem and I can only imagine how tight the budget must have been. It tried its best to be as realistic as possible but I can understand the need to move away from being too pure to keep the audiences entertained. At least it does it in a way that the message/s it tried to convey is/are not spoiled.

Bdf-56547

I was surprised by this film! Thank you to everyone who made this possible-- for sharing your talent and working so so hard. May this continue to spread to Filipinos who are oblivious of the reality of our country.
